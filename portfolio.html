CSC 4720 Portfolio

User & Problem Space Research

This is a document deliverable. Th document identifies the audience (users) you are serving, and the problem or situatin you are addressing. It is the result of research, sources including valid/reputable Web sources, conference papers, magazine, journal, or newspaper publications, research databases.

Skit/ Live Demo

My team mates and I performed a live demo/enactment of our problem and proposed solution. Props were encouraged. 3 minutes in duration

Interation Design

Document deliverable or output from prototyping software such as Axure. Wireframes and flow charts are used to describe how the user will interact with the system. Be prepared to present this in class for feedback from others

UI Design

Image files (png). Can be combined in a document deliverable. visual design mockups for our proposed solution. Using the wireframes, flows and user research for direction, we will make visual design mockups of key screens/states. Be prepared to present this in class .

Interactive Prototype

The deliverable is somewhat open to interpretation. If your solution is an app, then your interactive prototype can be the output from an interactive prototyping software such as Axure. Your prototype may incorporate a physical object you have constructed or 3D printed. Note, this is a physical/digital representation of your idea and how people interact wit it, not a working prototype of the technology behind it

Guerilla Usability test Findings

This is a document deliverable summarizing your usability test findings. Your team will conduct casual usability testing of your prototype with real people unassociated with your project team. You will gather data, asses the findings, and adjust (or not) your prototype accordingly.

Technology Paper

This is an article of 500 -1000 words in which you explore a topic in which technology is being used innovel ways in a specific field, industry, or purpose.

Final Presenation

For the final presentation, you will demonstrate your final prototype. Describe your audience, the problem you are solving, one of two of the challenges you faced, and findings from your usability test. You will turn in a min 5-slide summary of your project
